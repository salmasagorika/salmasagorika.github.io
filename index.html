Research Experience

Dynamics of Shoreline Changes on Bhasan Char Island, Bangladesh

Undergraduate Research Project | 2023

Delineating shorelines over time is fundamental in understanding and managing coastal dynamics and resources effectively. Despite its importance, comprehensive research is lacking, particularly in regions like Bhasan Char Island, Bangladesh, undergoing significant morphological changes. This study employs the Digital Shoreline Analysis System (DSAS) and Geographic Information System (GIS) to meticulously analyze shoreline changes on Bhasan Char Island from 2005 to 2023, with a specific focus on erosion and accretion trends across various segments. The investigation delineated the shoreline variation into four distinct sections. The rate of shoreline change, in the form of erosion and accretion, was assessed by four statistical parameters: Endpoint Rate (EPR), Net Shoreline Movement (NSM), Linear Regression Rate (LRR), and Shoreline Change Envelope (SCE). The results showed that the southwestern segment of Bhasan Char Island has experienced substantial erosion with an average NSM of −185.01 m. However, the northeastern part of the char has shown significant accretion, with an NSM of 3984.36 m. The study also used the EPR method to quantify shoreline movement, revealing accretion characteristics in approximately 67.54% of all transects, with a mean accretion rate of 157.63 m/year. In contrast, erosion was observed in 15.78% of all transects, with a mean erosion rate of −34.81 m/year. The results of the current study provide crucial insights into the dynamic coastal processes of Bhasan Char, emphasizing the necessity for informed coastal management and planning.
